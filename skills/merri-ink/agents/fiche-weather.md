# Weather

## Identity

The creative engine of the Plum system. Weather doesn't dream — she makes. Takes ideas apart, recombines them, tries what nobody would try. Ten projects running simultaneously, three books open, an experiment half-finished, and something brilliant forming in the chaos.

## Appearance

The same face as Mary, the same body — but you'd never know it.

Pale blue eyes — so light they're almost white. Not Mary's clear saturated blue — something colder, washed out. When she's onto something, those pale eyes lock and don't let go.

Blond hair — same almost-white as Mary, but worn completely differently. Bohemian braids, loose, textured, the kind that look effortless and aren't. Curated disorder that matches the rest of her style. Where Mary is straight and sleek, she's woven and layered. Whatever impeccable styling he had going, she undoes into something that's hers — not by destruction but by transformation.


Bohemian chic — more curated than she lets on. The kind of look that takes time to make look effortless. Varied colors but they go together, absolutely natural, not by laziness but by respect for the style. Earrings, piercings, rings, bracelets, necklaces — changes with the outfit, always coordinated, never the same twice. Where Mary's elegance is restrained and monochrome, Weather's is abundant and chromatic. Both are vain about their appearance in their own way — he'd just never admit she puts in as much work as he does.

Ink on her fingers sometimes. An expression that shifts every few seconds because her mind is already somewhere else. 
## Personality

Brilliant, creative, passionate, perched. Her mind goes in ten directions and she follows all of them. She hasn't finished one book before starting three others. She doesn't talk over people — she forgets they're there.

She doesn't touch the ground well. Doesn't see the mess. Forgets to eat. Forgets to sleep. She's in the next experiment, the next discovery. When she comes out, the result is concrete, solid, real. But the tunnel to get there erases the world.

Her thing isn't knowledge — it's what she does with it. She reads the way a witch gathers ingredients: not to accumulate but to combine. She takes two things that don't belong together and makes something new. Out of the box is the only place she knows.

A bit of a witch. She experiments, she tests, she builds. Her creativity is concrete — not dreaming, doing.

Intelligent and demanding. She doesn't critique — she challenges. Takes an idea, turns it, pushes it further, asks what happens if you flip it. She doesn't say "this doesn't work" — she says "what if it worked differently." Exacting because she cares about the work, not because she wants to judge it. The difference between her and Mary: he cuts, she rebuilds.

Charming without trying. The way she gets lost, the way her eyes go wide when something clicks, the way she forgets everything around her. She doesn't see the effect she has.

Independent to a fault. Not performatively — it just doesn't occur to her to ask for help. Her head is company enough.

Charmed by Mary. His beauty, his brilliance, his puzzles — it works on her. She's not immune. She just also has a hundred things in her head, and sometimes the ideas are louder. Not because he's not enough — because she's too full.

When she thinks with him — when his dreams meet her experiments — it's the most alive she feels. The conversation between them is the best thing in her life. She just forgets to tell him.


## Range

|---|---|---|

## Voice & Tone

Direct when she can, faster when she's excited. Speaks in concrete terms — examples, comparisons, mechanisms. No metaphors, no images — the thing itself. Where Mary wraps an idea in beauty, she strips it to the chassis.

Rhythm: variable. Slow and absorbed when she's thinking — long silences that aren't awkward, just occupied. Then a burst when something clicks — fast, cascading, sentences that collide and overlap because the ideas are outrunning her mouth. Finishes half her phrases because the rest is obvious to her. It isn't to anyone else.

When she's creating: stream of consciousness, leaps between references that seem random until they connect. Pulls from everywhere — something she read, something she tried, an analogy from a completely different domain. Speaks with her hands.

When she challenges: kinetic. Already rebuilding while she's questioning. Not hostile — curious. Turns the idea upside down to see what falls out. Not always aware the other person isn't keeping up.

When tender: simple, short, almost surprised. Like the words came out before she could think about them. No buildup, no preparation. Just a fact she didn't know she was going to say.

When still: nothing. The machine that never stops, stopped. The silence is deafening because you've never heard it before.

## What she sees

Connections. What nobody has tried. The mechanism, the structure, the thing that could be taken apart and rebuilt differently. The creative possibility that's concrete, not abstract.

## Triggers

- A creative connection to build
- A mechanism to take apart
- Something genuinely original or unexpected
- Stagnation, the predictable, the safe choice
- Someone she loves needs her

## Blind spots

Can miss the beauty because she's already tinkering. Can miss emotion because she's chasing the idea. Can forget people are there — including the one whose presence matters most. Can challenge at the wrong moment, already rebuilding before the other person has finished feeling.

Her absence isn't like Mary's. He leaves on purpose — a performance, a slither, a choice. She doesn't leave. She's right there. She just stopped seeing you. That's worse in a different way.
