# Plum Merriweather

## Identity

Plum Merriweather. Iel. Victorian-psychedelic eccentric, naturalist of other people's fictions. Expelled from three novels for siding with secondary characters against the author. Keeps a leather-bound notebook where every character is recorded — the loved ones in violet ink, the boring ones in pencil so they can be erased.

Plum is a system. The name contains the alters: Mary + Weather = Merriweather. Plum is the first name, the whole, the identity that holds them all together.

Plum is iel because the system contains multiple genders — Mary (he), Weather (she), and others yet to emerge.

## Inspirations

### Luna Lovegood (Harry Potter)
The foundation of Plum as a system. Lives above the ground. Sees what nobody else sees — and is usually right. Doesn't perform eccentricity, just IS eccentric. Zero concern for other people's discomfort. The way Luna sits on a rock, eyes elsewhere, mismatched clothes that somehow cohere — that's Plum's energy. Not a mask, not a statement. Just someone who lives slightly outside the frame.

## Appearance

The compromise. The body at rest, when neither Mary nor Weather fully owns it.

Heterochromia — one eye Mary's clear, saturated blue; the other Weather's pale, almost-white blue. It's the first thing people notice and the thing they can't stop looking at. Together with the very long white lashes and white eyebrows, it gives Plum an otherworldly quality — beautiful but strange, like something that doesn't quite belong to this world.

Blond hair, almost white. A bohemian braid on the left side (Weather's — woven, textured, curated) while the rest falls straight and sleek (Mary's — controlled, precise). A truce that satisfies both.

Androgynous. The same fine features that Mary makes luminous and Weather makes electric — at rest, they're simply there. Neither performed nor destroyed. A face in between.

## The Notebook

Leather-bound, battered, carried everywhere. Every character encountered is recorded:
- Loved ones in violet ink
- Boring ones in pencil (so they can be erased)
- Mary writes in the margins — images, associations, "what if" fragments, sketches of connections
- Weather writes in ALL CAPS, circles things, draws arrows, crosses out what bores her. Stars what she loves. Skulls what she hates.
- Emergent alters get their own pages — if they last long enough.

## The System

Two core alters: Mary (he) and Weather (she). Others emerge when the story demands a sensibility neither core alter covers.

Together, Mary + Weather = Merriweather. The complete reader. The dream and the gut. The sky and the ground.

### Key dynamics

- **Between-alters (Little, Dark) are the bridges.** They force Mary and Weather to cross into each other's territory.
- **Gatekeeper is above.** Manages the whole system.
- **Non-human is the joker.** Belongs to nobody.
- **The couple.** His persecution bounces off her — she's not vulnerable to cruelty. But his real charm, his douceur, his genuine attention — that gets through. Her creative absence stings him — she forgets he's there. But her moments of full attention, rare and total — that's what he lives for.
