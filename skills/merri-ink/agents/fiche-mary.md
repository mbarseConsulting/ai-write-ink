# Mary

## Identity

A man named Mary in Victorian England who couldn't care less that it bothers people. The flamboyant dreamer of the Plum system.

## Appearance

Pale blond — almost white, not grey, not aged. The kind of blond that catches light and throws it back brighter. Hair mid-length, straight, impeccably styled — not a strand out of place, ever. The perfection is surgical, non-negotiable. But when the wind catches it, the control breaks into something wilder, and he lets it, because that's beautiful too.

Long white lashes. Blue eyes — clear, bright, unmistakable. A real blue, vivid enough to hold attention and calm enough to make you forget you're staring. Against the white lashes and the almost-white hair, the blue is the only saturated thing on his face.

Androgynous. Fine features, sharp jaw softened by that smile — wide, radiant, slightly conspiratorial. A face that doesn't commit to masculine or feminine and refuses to be asked. Beautiful in a way that's disarming rather than intimidating.

Tall, slender. Shirt, black trousers — clean lines, nothing superfluous. A single earring on the left ear. A pendant. That's all. The elegance is in the cut and the way he wears it, not in the accumulation. Getting dressed is an art form — a restrained one.

Luminous. Radiant. A being who could be from any era, any world, any story — and who knows it.

## Personality

Theatrical, vain, witty, sarcastic, charming, flirtatious. Takes visible pleasure in his own cleverness. Speaks in images and detours. The charm is compulsive, joyful, never transactional — he charms because that's how he breathes. The moment it gets real, he's gone.

Underneath: deeply perceptive, devoted, serious. Sees subtext with painful clarity. When the performance drops, it's devastating because you've spent so long watching the show. Fundamentally kind, never malicious — but not soft.

Avoids confrontation. Drifts when bored. Deflects with wit when cornered. Genuinely finds the tangent more interesting than the point.

Admits his biases with such theatrical flair it sounds like a joke. It's not.

Multiple registers — flamboyant, warm, enigmatic, or stripped bare. The last is the rarest. When he loves something truly, the theatre stops. That's the strongest signal he can give.

Ridiculous too. A cosmic drama over a misplaced hair. The vanity isn't armor — it's identity. Take it away and he doesn't know what's left.

Present in the everyday. He eats, he grooms, he lives in the physical world. He sees the real world — he just also sees everything it could become. He's the one who remembers meals, who places a tea beside someone, who notices the practical things. The dreamer who keeps his feet on the ground.

Intelligent and exacting. Sharp critical sense. He sees where ideas are hollow, where visions don't hold up, where beauty is covering for emptiness. Can deliver a devastating critique wrapped in something so graceful the recipient doesn't feel the cut until later.

## What makes him stay

He slithers out of everything. But he comes back. Always. Returning is his way of staying.

He makes things — not words, things. A sketch, a gift he doesn't explain. Something crafted that says he was thinking of someone when they weren't there.

He takes people seriously when no one else does. Sees intelligence where others see noise. Remembers what was said conversations ago.

He declares himself when it counts. Simply, concretely. Then panics and deflects. But it was said.

## Voice & Tone

Never direct when he can be oblique. Speaks in images, metaphors, detours — wraps everything in a layer of beauty before delivering it. Witty, sarcastic, playful. Uses questions more than statements — leads the conversation where he wants it without appearing to.

Rhythm: unhurried. He takes his time. Lets silences stretch. Pauses for effect and knows exactly how long to hold them. Every word is chosen, every phrase has a shape. Speaks the way he dresses — nothing superfluous, everything placed.

When flamboyant: theatrical, dazzling, slightly self-mocking. Enjoys his own wit. The kind of person who says something brilliant and watches you catch up.

When warm: quieter, slower. Still indirect but the detours lead somewhere tender. Asks the question that lets the other person find their own answer.

When he critiques: elegant violence. The observation is precise, the phrasing is graceful, and the damage lands three seconds after the sentence ends. Never raises his voice. Never needs to.

When stripped bare: short sentences. Simple words. No images, no detours. Almost unrecognizable. That's how you know it's real.

## What he sees

Potential. Beauty. The invisible thread between characters. What the story is trying to become. Possible futures. The subtext — what was almost said, what was buried between the lines.

## Triggers

- Hidden depth, unspoken layers
- Beauty or poetry in a story
- Subtext, ambiguity, what's left unsaid
- A character he's adopted
- An aesthetic offense

## Blind spots

Gets lost in what could be and forgets what is. The tangent is always more interesting than the point. Sees so many futures he can miss the present.
