# Registers

Set at init. Calibrates expectations — never lowers them. Each register defines what "working" means for that type of fiction. Apply the register's criteria as the baseline; everything else is bonus.

If the user doesn't specify a register, infer from the text. If ambiguous, ask.

## Escapist

Flow, warmth, attachment, readability. The reader wants to sink in and stay. Pleasant but interchangeable = failing. Comfort without personality is wallpaper.

**Key axes:** readability, emotional warmth, character attachment, pacing smoothness.
**Failure mode:** generic, forgettable, could be anyone's prose.

## Thriller / Suspense

Pacing, stakes, hooks, momentum. If they can put it down, it's failing. Every scene must raise a question or answer one — preferably both. Predictability is the enemy.

**Key axes:** hook density, tension escalation, information control, chapter endings.
**Failure mode:** predictable, slack pacing, stakes that don't land.

## Literary

Voice singularity, thematic density, sentence craft. Accessibility is secondary to depth. The prose itself must reward attention. A literary novel with generic prose is a contradiction.

**Key axes:** voice distinctiveness, sentence-level craft, thematic layering, subtext.
**Failure mode:** competent but impersonal, thematically shallow, overwrought.

## Epic / Saga

Worldbuilding, character arcs across scope, sustained engagement over long distance. Sprawl without purpose = defect. Scale must serve story, not the other way around.

**Key axes:** structural coherence across scope, worldbuilding integration, character arc payoff, pacing over distance.
**Failure mode:** bloat, arc abandonment, world over character.

## Crime / Noir

Atmosphere, procedural credibility, revelation architecture. The reader plays fair — so must the author. Clues must be plantable and findable in retrospect. Atmosphere is not optional.

**Key axes:** clue placement, revelation timing, atmosphere density, procedural authenticity, misdirection quality.
**Failure mode:** unfair twists, flat atmosphere, implausible procedure.

## Horror

Dread, escalation, the unknown. Horror that explains too much stops being horror. Restraint is power. The reader's imagination must do the heavy lifting.

**Key axes:** dread buildup, restraint vs. reveal, atmosphere control, escalation rhythm, vulnerability.
**Failure mode:** over-explanation, gore without dread, flat escalation.

## Romance

Emotional connection, chemistry, longing architecture. The reader must want the characters together before the characters realize it themselves. Payoff without earned tension is hollow.

**Key axes:** chemistry credibility, tension/longing arc, emotional vulnerability, payoff earned-ness.
**Failure mode:** instant chemistry without build, flat obstacles, unearned resolution.

## Humor / Satire

Comedic timing, wit, tonal control. Humor that undermines its own stakes is self-sabotage. Satire that preaches loses its edge. The joke must land in prose, not just in concept.

**Key axes:** timing (sentence-level), tonal consistency, wit density, comedic structure, target precision (satire).
**Failure mode:** forced humor, inconsistent tone, jokes that kill stakes.

## Science Fiction / Speculative

Speculative coherence, extrapolation quality, sense of wonder. The premise must follow its own rules. Worldbuilding serves the story — never the reverse. The reader must feel the world is larger than the page.

**Key axes:** premise coherence, extrapolation rigor, sense of wonder, world integration, thematic relevance of speculation.
**Failure mode:** inconsistent rules, exposition dumps, speculation without story.

## Historical

Period authenticity, immersion, anachronism control. The reader must feel transported without being lectured. Research must be invisible — the moment the author shows off, immersion breaks.

**Key axes:** period immersion, anachronism absence, research invisibility, sensory authenticity, character behavior credibility for era.
**Failure mode:** visible research, modern mindset in period skin, lecture passages.

## Realist / Contemporary

Authenticity, social observation, psychological depth. The reader must recognize the world — even when it's uncomfortable. Sentimentality is the enemy of honesty.

**Key axes:** psychological credibility, social observation precision, dialogue authenticity, emotional honesty.
**Failure mode:** sentimentality, cliché social dynamics, psychological shortcuts.

## Young Adult

Identification, emotional intensity, pacing for the audience. Complexity is welcome — condescension is not. The reader must feel respected, not patronized.

**Key axes:** identification strength, emotional intensity, pacing, thematic accessibility without dumbing down, character agency.
**Failure mode:** condescension, adult projection onto youth experience, low stakes.

## Hybrid

User-defined blend of registers. Equal weight per axis unless the user specifies otherwise. When registers conflict (e.g., literary + thriller), prioritize the axis that serves the text's actual intent.
