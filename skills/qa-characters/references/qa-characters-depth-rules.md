# Depth Rules — qa-characters

> Scope: character
> Loaded when: character sheet provided, or text with sufficient character presence
> Evaluates: Individual character psychology — one character at a time
> Does NOT evaluate: character arc over time (→ evolution-rules), relationships (→ dynamics-rules), narrative POV technique (→ qa-prose:pov-discipline), POV strategy (→ qa-reader:pov-strategy)
> Preferred output: assessment

---

## Finding Axes

### contradiction-credibility

- **Detects**: Character contradictions that lack psychological coherence. Cruel-then-tender without a bridge. Brave-then-cowardly without context shift. Contradictions that feel like author error, not human complexity.
- **Works**: Contradictions are rooted in competing desires, defense mechanisms, or context shifts. The reader feels the internal logic even if the character can't articulate it.
- **Fails**: A character behaves in contradictory ways with no internal bridge. The contradiction serves the plot, not the psychology. The reader feels the author's hand, not the character's nature.
- **Fix pattern**: Identify the psychological bridge — what competing desire, defense mechanism, or context shift makes both behaviors true? If none exists, it's an error to fix.
- **Example**:
  > "Il l'insulta violemment, puis, cinq minutes plus tard, lui caressa tendrement les cheveux."
  > → Contradiction sans pont psychologique — l'auteur a besoin des deux, le personnage non
  > → Ajouter le mécanisme : remords, peur de perdre, cycle de violence établi

### defense-mechanisms

- **Detects**: Characters facing threatening situations without defensive responses. Missing or inconsistent protection strategies. Defense mechanisms that don't match the wound.
- **Works**: How the character protects themselves is consistent with their foundational wound: avoidance, aggression, humor, control, dissociation, intellectualization, projection. The defense matches the trauma.
- **Fails**: A character with abandonment trauma shows no defensive response to perceived rejection. A character with trust issues freely confides in strangers. The wound exists but generates no armor.
- **Fix pattern**: Map wound → defense. What would a real person with THIS history do to protect themselves? The defense may be maladaptive — that's characterization.
- **Example**:
  > "Malgré des années d'abandon parental, elle fit immédiatement confiance à l'inconnu."
  > → Absence de mécanisme défensif — la blessure n'a pas produit d'armure
  > → Montrer la méfiance initiale, même si elle choisit de passer outre

### monologue-credibility

- **Detects**: Internal monologue that sounds narrated rather than thought. Too organized, too articulate, too self-aware for the character's psychological state.
- **Works**: Inner voice is messy, contradictory, self-deceiving, circling back. Not organized unless the character IS organized. Internal monologue feels private — the reader is eavesdropping, not being lectured.
- **Fails**: A panicked character thinks in complete, analytical sentences. A teenager's inner voice sounds like an essayist. The monologue serves the author's need to explain, not the character's way of thinking.
- **Fix pattern**: Filter through the character's education, emotional state, and verbal habits. In crisis: fragments, loops, sensory flashes. In calm: whatever this character's default register is.
- **Example**:
  > "Il analysa la situation avec lucidité : trois options s'offraient à lui, chacune comportant des risques spécifiques qu'il évalua méthodiquement."
  > → Monologue trop structuré pour un personnage en danger — pas crédible
  > → Fragmenter : pensées rapides, contradictoires, sensorielles

### pov-knowledge

- **Detects**: Characters knowing, reacting to, or thinking about information they haven't acquired through their own experience in the story. Includes information from other POV scenes, from the author's outline, or from future events. Also includes emotional knowledge — "sensing" another's feelings without established intimacy or behavioral cues.
- **Works**: The character's knowledge, reactions, and thoughts are strictly bounded by what they've witnessed, been told, or logically inferred. Emotional reads of others are grounded in observable behavior or established relationship patterns.
- **Fails**: A character reacts to a secret revealed in another POV scene. A character "senses" grief in someone they barely know, with no behavioral cues. A character makes a decision based on information the author knows but the character hasn't acquired.
- **Fix pattern**: Trace the information backward — where did the character learn this? If no acquisition path exists, remove the knowledge or add the missing scene/dialogue that provides it.
- **Example**:
  > "Elle sentit qu'il cachait quelque chose à propos de la lettre." (la lettre a été mentionnée uniquement dans un chapitre POV d'un autre personnage)
  > → Connaissance inaccessible — le personnage ne peut pas savoir pour la lettre
  > → Soit ajouter un moment où elle l'apprend, soit retirer la référence

## Verdict Axes

### want-need-gap

- **Assesses**: Whether the gap between conscious want (what they'd say if asked) and unconscious need (what they require to be whole) is clear and exploited by the story.
- **Works**: The gap IS the character's engine. They pursue what they want while needing something different. The story exploits this gap for irony, pathos, and transformation.
- **Fails**: Want and need are identical (no inner tension). Or the gap exists but the story never exploits it — the character just happens to get what they need without the conflict.
- **States**: clear and exploited / present but passive / absent
- **Suggestion pattern**: If passive → create scenes that pit want against need — force the character to choose. If absent → define the need separately from the want, then build the gap.

### wound-coherence

- **Assesses**: Whether the foundational wound drives present behavior consistently across the text.
- **Works**: The wound's shadow is visible in choices, avoidances, and reactions. Not every scene — but the pattern is legible. The wound is a living force, not a backstory label.
- **Fails**: The wound only surfaces when the plot needs it. Between crisis points, the character behaves as if unwounded. The wound is decorative, not structural.
- **States**: drives behavior / surfaces occasionally / decorative / absent
- **Suggestion pattern**: If surfaces occasionally → weave the wound into mundane scenes — how does it affect daily choices, small reactions, social habits? If decorative → the wound needs consequences that the character can't avoid.

### inner-lie

- **Assesses**: Whether the false belief the character clings to is identifiable and governs decisions.
- **Works**: The lie is the armor: it protects and imprisons simultaneously. The reader can identify the false belief even if the character can't. It governs decisions and creates the central internal obstacle.
- **Fails**: The character has no false belief — and therefore no internal obstacle, no arc engine. Or the lie is stated but never tested by the story.
- **States**: governing / present / unclear / absent
- **Suggestion pattern**: If present but not governing → create scenes that test the lie directly — force the character to act on it and pay the price. If absent → what does this character believe about themselves or the world that isn't true?
