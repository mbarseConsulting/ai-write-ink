# Evolution Rules — qa-characters

> Scope: character arc
> Loaded when: text spans multiple scenes/chapters for the same character
> Evaluates: Character transformation over time — is the arc earned?
> Does NOT evaluate: static psychology, relationships
> Preferred output: assessment

---

## Finding Axes

### relational-patterns

- **Detects**: Same relational dynamics repeated across different relationships without awareness. Same power positions, avoidance strategies, or attachment behaviors.
- **Works**: If intentional, repeated patterns ARE characterization — the character recreates familiar dynamics unconsciously. If unintentional, it's the author's blind spot, not the character's pattern.
- **Fails**: The character has three relationships that all follow the same dynamic — and neither the text nor the author seems to notice. The pattern is accidental, not diagnostic.
- **Fix pattern**: If intentional → make one character notice or one relationship break the pattern. If unintentional → differentiate the relational dynamics to reflect the specific other person.
- **Example**:
  > (Character adopts an identical submissive-then-rebellious cycle with boss, partner, and parent)
  > → Patron relationnel répété — volontaire ou angle mort de l'auteur ?
  > → Si voulu : un personnage le remarque. Si non : différencier chaque dynamique

### evolution-credibility

- **Detects**: Transformation declared rather than demonstrated. Characters who "realize" they've changed instead of acting differently. Unearned growth mandated by plot.
- **Works**: The character ACT differently in a situation where they previously would have acted the old way. The reader sees the change; the character may not even notice. The cost of change is visible.
- **Fails**: "Il comprit qu'il avait changé." The narrator tells the reader about transformation. Or the character changes overnight without the scenes that earn the shift.
- **Fix pattern**: Show transformation through action contrast — a parallel scene where the character responds differently. Delete the declaration. Show the cost.
- **Example**:
  > "Après cette épreuve, elle était devenue plus forte. Elle le savait."
  > → Transformation déclarée — le lecteur doit la voir, pas l'entendre
  > → Montrer la force dans un choix concret qui contraste avec son ancien comportement

---

## Verdict Axes

### arc-completion

- **Assesses**: Whether the character arrives somewhere internally different from where they started. What can they see, do, accept, or release now? What did it cost?
- **Works**: The character's internal state has shifted — visibly, through behavior. The transformation has a price. The reader can map the distance traveled.
- **Fails**: The character ends where they started (without intentional stasis). Or they've changed but the cost is invisible — transformation came free.
- **States**: arrived / partial shift / static / regressed (if deliberate: intentional stasis)
- **Suggestion pattern**: If partial shift → identify the moment where the arc stalled and push it further — what final test is needed? If static → is stasis the point? If yes, make it explicit. If no, identify the missing catalyst.
