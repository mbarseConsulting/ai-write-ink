# Macro Rules — qa-reader

> Scope: macro
> Loaded when: text >= multiple chapters or full manuscript
> Evaluates: Manuscript-level reading experience — the book as a whole
> Does NOT evaluate: voice singularity (see verdict axis below)
> Preferred output: assessment
> Traversal: Map the entire manuscript structure before diagnosing. Create an energy/engagement map across ALL chapters/sections. Dead zones in the middle are the most common blind spot.

---

## Finding Axes

### tonal-consistency

- **Detects**: Accidental tonal shifts. A comedy that turns dark without preparation. A thriller that wanders into philosophical essay. Also: tonal flatness — one register so rigid it becomes monotonous. And: gradual tonal drift — the book slowly becomes something different without the author noticing.
- **Works**: Tonal shifts are deliberate and prepared. The tonal range is wide enough to create contrast (light moments in dark books, gravity in comedies) without breaking the contract. Register stays consistent within the narrator's voice unless a shift is earned.
- **Fails**: The tone breaks without warning. The reader feels whiplash. Or: the tone never shifts — the reader goes numb. Or: chapter 15 feels like a different book than chapter 1 without the author seeming to notice.
- **Fix pattern**: If intentional, add preparation — plant tonal seeds earlier. If accidental, align with the surrounding register. If drifting, identify where drift began — commit to the evolution or correct it. If flat, introduce controlled variation.
- **Mechanism markers**: Register words that clash with surroundings. Sudden shifts from scene to essay, action to meditation, humor to tragedy without transitional beats. The "soundtrack test": if the scene were a film, would the music shift feel motivated or jarring?
- **Example**:
  > (Thriller qui inclut soudain une meditation philosophique de 3 pages sur le libre arbitre)
  > → Rupture tonale — essai philosophique dans un thriller sans preparation. The reader signed up for tension, not a lecture.
  > → Integrer la reflexion dans l'action : le personnage AGIT la question philosophique au lieu de la narrer.

### narrative-momentum

- **Detects**: Whether the story accumulates power over time or resets with each scene/chapter. Accumulation vs. episodic flatness. Does chapter 15 hit harder BECAUSE of chapters 1-14? Or could the chapters be rearranged without the reader noticing?
- **Works**: Later scenes gain power from earlier ones. Callbacks land. Emotional investments pay off. The reader's knowledge of characters makes each new scene richer. The story is greater than the sum of its parts. Events are connected by "and therefore" (causal), not "and then" (sequential).
- **Fails**: Each scene starts from zero. Previous events have no visible effect on current scenes. Characters don't carry their history. The reader could skip chapters without losing context — because context doesn't accumulate.
- **Fix pattern**: Identify what should echo from earlier scenes into later ones. Add callbacks — not repetitions, but moments where earlier events visibly affect current behavior, choices, or emotional responses. Create through-lines that reward the invested reader.
- **Mechanism markers**: The "causation test" — are major beats connected by "and therefore" or "and then"? If "and then," momentum is episodic. The "scar test" — do previous events leave visible marks on subsequent behavior? If characters reset between chapters, momentum fails. The "reorder test" — could chapters be rearranged without the reader noticing? If yes, momentum is episodic.
- **Genre note**: Thriller = escalating stakes and tightening pressure; each chapter raises stakes from the previous. Romance = deepening intimacy and rising barriers; emotional stakes grow with each encounter. Literary = accumulating layers of meaning that build. Horror = eroding safety; each chapter removes another layer of protection.
- **Example**:
  > (Le personnage fait un sacrifice important au chapitre 5, puis se comporte au chapitre 10 comme si rien ne s'etait passe)
  > → Pas d'accumulation — les evenements n'ont pas de poids dans la suite.
  > → Le sacrifice devrait colorer chaque decision qui suit — montrer les cicatrices.

### vivid-continuous-dream

- **Detects**: Anything that breaks the reader's immersive trance state (John Gardner). The reader is "dreaming" the story — experiencing it as if lived. Dream-breakers include: authorial intrusion (the author's personality showing through the narrator), logical impossibilities (a character couldn't know this, a door was locked three pages ago), stylistic inconsistencies, meta-commentary, anachronisms, information the POV character doesn't have, and any moment where the reader becomes aware of the AUTHOR rather than the STORY.
- **Works**: The reader never thinks about the author. The story world is consistent, the narrator's voice is reliable, the physical logic holds. When the prose is at its best, the reader forgets they're reading.
- **Fails**: A character references information from a scene they weren't in. The prose style shifts without motivation. The narrator makes a joke that belongs to the author, not the story world. A physical impossibility. An anachronism. Any moment where the reader thinks "wait —".
- **Fix pattern**: Identify the dream-breaker type. Logical: fix continuity. Authorial intrusion: remove the author's voice, let the character's carry. Stylistic: align with surrounding register. Meta-commentary: does it serve the story? Every dream-break is a reader-trust withdrawal.
- **Mechanism markers**: Characters knowing things they weren't told. Objects appearing/disappearing between scenes. Physical impossibilities (two-handed action while carrying something). Narrator commentary that belongs to the author ("comme on le sait"). Style shifts within a chapter without motivation. The "who's talking" test: is this the character's thought or the author's opinion? If the author's, it's an intrusion.
- **Example**:
  > "Il sourit en repensant a ce que Marie lui avait dit — mais Marie ne lui avait rien dit, pas dans cette scene."
  > → Rupture logique — le personnage sait quelque chose qu'il ne devrait pas savoir.
  > → Verifier la coherence informationnelle : le personnage ne peut reagir qu'a ce qu'il a vecu dans le texte.

### reader-fatigue

- **Detects**: Sustained passages that tax the reader's cognitive budget without proportional reward. Dense exposition, repetitive emotional beats, unbroken interiority, relentless action without breathing room. The reader is willing to work hard — but the effort must pay off.
- **Works**: Demanding passages are followed by easier ones. Dense information is broken by action or emotion. Long interiority is broken by event. The effort-to-reward ratio is balanced.
- **Fails**: 20 pages of worldbuilding exposition. Five consecutive chapters of interior monologue. Three chapters of action with no pause. The reader's attention is exhausted because the effort doesn't pay off.
- **Fix pattern**: Map cognitive demand across the manuscript. After every demanding passage, give the reader something easy and rewarding — a scene that moves, an emotional beat that lands, a revelation that justifies the work. Alternate density with clarity, complexity with simplicity.
- **Mechanism markers**: Create a demand map: for each chapter, rate cognitive effort (H/M/L) and emotional reward (H/M/L). Effort H + Reward L sustained for more than 2 chapters = fatigue risk. The "reward test": after every dense or demanding passage, is there a payoff within 2-3 pages? If the reader works for 10 pages without reward, fatigue sets in. Also: repetitive emotional cycling (the same push-pull without escalation) = a specific form of fatigue.
- **Genre note**: SF/Fantasy vulnerable through worldbuilding exposition. Thriller through relentless action. Literary through sustained interiority. Romance through repetitive emotional cycling (same push-pull without escalation).
- **Example**:
  > (Quinze pages d'explication du systeme magique, aucune action ni ancrage emotionnel)
  > → Fatigue lecteur — effort cognitif sans recompense emotionnelle.
  > → Fragmenter l'exposition, l'integrer dans des scenes d'action ou de conflit.

## Verdict Axes

### narrative-arc

- **Assesses**: Whether the story goes somewhere. Stakes escalation, momentum, structural drive.
- **Works**: The second half raises questions the first half couldn't have asked. Events are connected by "and therefore" (causal) not just "and then" (sequential). Complications escalate — each harder than the last. The trajectory feels both inevitable and surprising.
- **Fails**: The story flattens in the middle, circles without progressing, or collapses in the final act. No escalation. Events are sequential, not causal.
- **States**: drives forward / stalls / circles / collapses
- **Suggestion pattern**: If stalls → inject irreversible complication. If circles → map the repetitive loop, break with irreversible change. If collapses → trace the climax backward to find missing setup.
- **Diagnostic method**: Stakes map — what's at risk in each act? If stakes don't escalate, arc is flat. Irreversibility map — a story that can "reset" has no forward drive. The "and therefore" test — is each major beat caused by the previous one?

### singular-voice

- **Assesses**: Whether the prose has a voice the reader would recognize blind. Not correctness — distinctiveness.
- **Works**: A paragraph from this book, stripped of context, would be identifiable. The voice has patterns, textures, obsessions that belong to THIS narrator. The reader is in the presence of a specific sensibility.
- **Fails**: The prose is competent but interchangeable. It could belong to any of a hundred novels. No signature rhythm, no characteristic imagery, no verbal personality.
- **States**: distinctive / competent / generic / absent
- **Suggestion pattern**: If competent → identify the moments where voice breaks through (they exist) and analyze what makes them distinctive. Scale those qualities. If generic → the voice is hiding behind convention — find the author's natural oddities and amplify them.

### thematic-resonance

- **Assesses**: Whether the book is about something beneath the plot. Theme felt, not stated. McKee: the story argues its controlling idea through dramatized events, not narrator statements.
- **Works**: Scenes, characters, and choices echo a central question without naming it. The reader senses the depth. Characters embody different positions on the thematic question through choices, not speeches. Theme emerges from the story's logic — events ARGUE the theme.
- **Fails**: Pure plot with no meaning beyond surface. Or: theme hammered through dialogue and narration. Or: the story's events contradict its stated theme.
- **States**: resonates / present but shallow / absent
- **Suggestion pattern**: If shallow → weave into character choices, not narrator statements. If absent → what question does this story implicitly ask? Build scenes that embody it.
- **Diagnostic method**: The "about test" — "This book is about [plot], but it's REALLY about [theme]." If you can't complete the second half, resonance is absent. If theme only visible in dialogue, it's shallow.

### global-engagement

- **Assesses**: The ultimate reader verdict — after 50 pages, would you choose to keep reading? Requires surveying the ENTIRE manuscript.
- **Works**: Genuine desire to know what happens. No sustained dead zones. The "just one more chapter" effect.
- **Fails**: Reading out of obligation. Specific dead zones identifiable. The reader can stop at natural stopping points and never picks it back up.
- **States**: compulsive / willing / dutiful / abandoned
- **Suggestion pattern**: If willing → scale the mechanism that works in the strongest section. If dutiful → identify dead zone(s) and diagnose why (missing stakes? character change? information tension? microtension?). If abandoned → the contract failed — rebuild from the opening.
- **Diagnostic method**: Create an engagement curve — rate each chapter 1-5 for "would I keep reading?" Identify valleys. For each: pacing problem (no tension), relevance problem (wrong content), or execution problem (weak prose)?
