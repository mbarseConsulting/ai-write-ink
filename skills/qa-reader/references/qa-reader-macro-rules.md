# Macro Rules — qa-reader

> Scope: macro
> Loaded when: text >= multiple chapters or full manuscript
> Evaluates: Manuscript-level reading experience — the book as a whole
> Does NOT evaluate: voice singularity
> Preferred output: assessment

---

## Finding Axes

### tonal-consistency

- **Detects**: Accidental tonal shifts. A comedy that turns dark without preparation. A thriller that wanders into philosophical essay. Tonal breaks without justification.
- **Works**: Tonal shifts are deliberate and prepared. The reader feels the shift as intentional — a tonal change with setup is a feature, not a flaw.
- **Fails**: The tone breaks without warning or preparation. The reader feels whiplash. The shift serves the author's impulse, not the story's need.
- **Fix pattern**: If the shift is intentional, add preparation — plant tonal seeds earlier. If accidental, align the passage with the surrounding register.
- **Example**:
  > (Thriller that suddenly includes a 3-page philosophical meditation on free will)
  > → Rupture tonale — essai philosophique dans un thriller sans préparation
  > → Intégrer la réflexion dans l'action, ou la préparer par des touches plus tôt

## Verdict Axes

### narrative-arc

- **Assesses**: Whether the story goes somewhere. Stakes escalation, momentum, structural drive toward confrontation.
- **Works**: The second half raises questions the first half couldn't have asked. Momentum builds. The trajectory feels both inevitable and surprising.
- **Fails**: The story flattens in the middle, circles without progressing, or collapses in the final act. No escalation. The end doesn't feel like a destination.
- **States**: drives forward / stalls / circles / collapses
- **Suggestion pattern**: If stalls → identify the dead zone and inject a complication. If circles → map the repetitive loop, break it with irreversible change. If collapses → the climax lacks setup — trace what's missing backward.

### thematic-resonance

- **Assesses**: Whether the book is about something beneath the plot. Theme felt, not stated.
- **Works**: Scenes, characters, and choices echo a central question without naming it. The reader senses the depth. The book lingers after finishing.
- **Fails**: The book is pure plot — things happen, but they don't mean anything beyond their surface. Or the theme is hammered explicitly through dialogue and narration.
- **States**: resonates / present but shallow / absent
- **Suggestion pattern**: If shallow → identify the thematic thread and weave it into character choices, not narrator statements. If absent → what question does this story implicitly ask? Build scenes that embody it.

### global-engagement

- **Assesses**: The ultimate reader verdict — after 50 pages, would you choose to keep reading? Includes identifying dead zones: where does engagement drop, where does the reader consider putting the book down?
- **Works**: Not out of duty or professional interest — out of genuine desire to know what happens. The book has earned its reader's time. Engagement holds across the full manuscript — no sustained dead zones.
- **Fails**: The reader continues out of obligation. Or puts the book down. The prose is competent but the experience is forgettable. Specific dead zones identifiable (e.g., "chapters 12-16 sag").
- **States**: compulsive / willing / dutiful / abandoned
- **Suggestion pattern**: If willing → identify what separates the strongest section from the weakest — scale the mechanism that works. If dutiful → identify the dead zone(s) by chapter/section — diagnose why engagement drops there and what's missing (stakes, character change, information). If abandoned → the contract failed — return to opening and rebuild the reader's investment.
