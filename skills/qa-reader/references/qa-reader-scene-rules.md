# Scene Rules — qa-reader

> Scope: meso
> Loaded when: text >= 1 complete scene
> Evaluates: Scene-level engagement — does the scene work as a unit of change?
> Preferred output: inline or diagnostic

---

## Finding Axes

### tension

- **Detects**: Scenes without stakes. Nothing at risk — no safety, trust, identity, secret, or relationship under pressure. The reader has no reason to wonder what happens next.
- **Works**: Something is at stake. The reader wants to know the outcome. Tension can be quiet (will she say it?) or loud (will he survive?). The engine is present.
- **Fails**: Characters interact pleasantly without friction. A meeting occurs but nothing is at risk. The scene could be removed and the reader would miss nothing.
- **Fix pattern**: Identify what's at stake for the POV character. If nothing — add a want, a fear, or a secret. Give the scene an engine.
- **Example**:
  > "Ils déjeunèrent ensemble et discutèrent de la pluie et du beau temps."
  > → Scène sans enjeu — rien en jeu, rien à résoudre
  > → Donner à un personnage un objectif caché ou une information à révéler

### scene-arc

- **Detects**: Scenes where nothing changes. The world is the same at the end as at the beginning — no relationship shift, no revelation, no decision, no belief cracking.
- **Works**: The scene starts somewhere and ends somewhere different. Something shifts: a relationship, an understanding, a balance of power, a piece of knowledge.
- **Fails**: Characters meet, talk, and separate without anything being different. No turn. It's a passage, not a scene.
- **Fix pattern**: Identify the turn. If there's no turn, add one: a revelation, a decision, a shift in power. The scene earns its existence through change.
- **Example**:
  > (Entire scene of two characters catching up on news with no shift)
  > → Pas d'arc — les personnages sont au même point au début et à la fin
  > → Introduire un basculement : une info change la donne, un non-dit éclate

### scene-economy

- **Detects**: Scenes that start too early (arrival, greeting, settling in) or end too late (cooldown, departure, reflection on what just happened). Transitions that should be skipped.
- **Works**: Start late, exit early. Enter at the moment of friction. Leave before the cooldown. The reader fills in transitions.
- **Fails**: The scene opens with the character driving to the meeting, parking, entering, greeting. The scene ends with goodbyes, departure, and internal reflection on what happened.
- **Fix pattern**: Cut the approach and the aftermath. Start at the first moment of friction. End on the strongest beat.
- **Example**:
  > "Elle gara la voiture, vérifia son maquillage dans le rétroviseur, prit son sac et marcha vers l'entrée du restaurant."
  > → Approche — on attend la scène, rien ne se passe
  > → Couper : démarrer au moment où la tension commence

### microtension

- **Detects**: Pages without micro-mysteries. No unanswered question, no detail that doesn't fit, no mismatch between what's said and done. The reader's unconscious engagement drops.
- **Works**: On every page: a question left open, a detail that intrigues, a gap between words and behavior. The reader's need to resolve micro-mysteries sustains reading between major plot points.
- **Fails**: A page of competent prose with no hooks. Everything makes sense. Nothing pulls forward. The reader can put the book down here.
- **Fix pattern**: Plant a small mystery on every page — a reaction that doesn't match, a detail the character notices but doesn't understand, an offhand remark with hidden weight.
- **Example**:
  > (Two pages of conversation where every statement is taken at face value)
  > → Aucune microtension — pas de question ouverte, pas de décalage
  > → Ajouter un non-dit, un regard inexpliqué, une réponse qui ne colle pas

### character-agency

- **Detects**: POV character as passive observer. Things happen around them. They watch, react, are moved by events. No choices, no initiative, no action that drives the scene.
- **Works**: The POV character drives the scene. Makes choices. Takes action. Even in reactive scenes, they CHOOSE how to react. The reader follows a protagonist, not a passenger.
- **Fails**: The character watches a conversation between others. Events happen TO them. Their only verbs are perception verbs: voir, entendre, sentir, remarquer.
- **Fix pattern**: Give the character a decision to make. Replace observation with action. Even small choices (to stay, to speak, to lie) create agency.
- **Example**:
  > "Elle observa la dispute sans intervenir, se contentant d'écouter les arguments de chacun."
  > → Passivité — le personnage regarde, le lecteur décroche
  > → Lui donner un choix : intervenir, partir, mentir, choisir un camp

### pacing

- **Detects**: Consecutive scenes at the same intensity level. All crisis, or all reflection. No alternation between tension and rest. The rhythm doesn't serve the story.
- **Works**: Tension and rest alternate. Acceleration before climax, breathing room after intensity. The rhythm matches the narrative need.
- **Fails**: Three consecutive high-tension scenes (reader exhausted). Three consecutive reflective scenes (reader bored). No variation in energy.
- **Fix pattern**: Map scene energy across the sequence. Insert a contrasting scene where intensity flatlines. Give the reader permission to breathe — then hit again.
- **Example**:
  > (Three consecutive fight/chase sequences with no downbeat)
  > → Rythme — trois scènes haute tension consécutives, épuisement lecteur
  > → Insérer un temps de respiration entre deux scènes d'intensité
