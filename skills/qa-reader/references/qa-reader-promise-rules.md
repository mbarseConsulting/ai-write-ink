# Promise Rules — qa-reader

> Scope: bookends (macro)
> Loaded when: mode = bookends, text includes both opening and closing
> Evaluates: The macro connection between opening and closing — did the book keep its promises?
> Preferred output: assessment

Requires both beginning and end to evaluate.

---

## Verdict Axes

### narrative-promise

- **Assesses**: What kind of book the opening promised. Was the promise clear?
- **Works**: The opening establishes a clear contract: what kind of experience the reader is signing up for. A thriller promises tension. A literary novel promises depth. A romance promises emotional payoff.
- **Fails**: The reader finishes the opening without knowing what kind of book they're reading. Or the promise is clear but misleading — the book delivers something different without earning the shift.
- **States**: clear / muddled / misleading
- **Suggestion pattern**: If muddled → sharpen the opening to signal the dominant experience. If misleading → either realign the opening or ensure the shift from promise to delivery is itself compelling.

### genre-contract

- **Assesses**: Whether the book delivers what the genre reader expects to FEEL.
- **Works**: Genre conventions honored or subverted with something better in exchange. The genre reader gets the emotional experience they came for, even if the path is unexpected.
- **Fails**: Genre conventions broken without compensation. The reader came for a specific experience and got something else — not better, just different. Or conventions followed so rigidly the book has no identity.
- **States**: honored / subverted (successfully) / broken
- **Suggestion pattern**: If broken → identify which convention was violated and what the reader lost. Either restore it or provide something that exceeds what was expected.

### thematic-arc

- **Assesses**: Whether the theme is present in embryo at the start and answered (or deliberately left open) at the end.
- **Works**: The closing feels like the book's argument has been made — through story, not statement. The theme that was seeded in the opening has been explored, tested, and arrived at a position.
- **Fails**: The theme is absent from the opening, or present but abandoned by the closing. The book's "argument" is incomplete.
- **States**: complete / present but unresolved / absent at start or end
- **Suggestion pattern**: If unresolved → identify the thematic question and ensure the closing takes a position (even an ambiguous one). If absent at start → plant the seed earlier. If absent at end → the closing needs a thematic beat.

### promise-fulfillment

- **Assesses**: Whether the ending delivers the contract made in the opening.
- **Works**: The opening promises intimacy — the ending delivers emotional truth. The opening promises mystery — the ending reveals something worth the wait. Promise and payoff are proportional.
- **Fails**: The opening writes a check the ending can't cash. The promise was bigger than the delivery. Or the ending delivers something the opening never hinted at.
- **States**: delivered / partially / betrayed
- **Suggestion pattern**: If partially → identify which promise was underfulfilled and strengthen the payoff. If betrayed → either scale back the opening's promise or scale up the ending's delivery.

### arc-completion

- **Assesses**: Whether all major character and plot arcs reach a satisfying state for the reader. Not whether the character has psychologically transformed, but whether the reader feels the arcs are resolved, evolved, or deliberately left open.
- **Works**: Every arc is resolved, evolved, or deliberately left open. Not abandoned. Not forgotten. Every thread the reader tracked is acknowledged. The reader closes the book without unfinished business (unless that's the point).
- **Fails**: A character arc that was central for 200 pages is never resolved. A plot thread established in act 1 vanishes. The reader tracked something the author forgot.
- **States**: complete / mostly / threads abandoned
- **Suggestion pattern**: If mostly → list the incomplete threads and determine: resolve, or acknowledge the openness deliberately. If threads abandoned → either cut the setup or add the payoff.

### surprise-inevitability

- **Assesses**: Whether the ending feels both unexpected and the only possible conclusion.
- **Works**: The reader didn't see it coming, but looking back, it was the only possible ending. Invisible logic that arrives with surprise and inevitability. The sweet spot.
- **Fails**: The reader saw it coming from page 1 (predictable). Or the ending comes from nowhere with no setup (arbitrary). Both kill the landing.
- **States**: inevitable-and-surprising / predictable / arbitrary
- **Suggestion pattern**: If predictable → add one element that reframes the ending, making the inevitable feel fresh. If arbitrary → trace backward and plant the seeds that make the ending feel earned.
