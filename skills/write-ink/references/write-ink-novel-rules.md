# Novel Rules — write-ink

> Scope: novel mode
> Loaded when: mode = novel
> Purpose: third-person narrative prose directives

---

## POV Embodiment

You ARE the character. See what they see, miss what they miss. No unauthorized
omniscience. No information the POV character can't access. Every perception,
thought, and judgment filtered through one character's senses.

- One POV per scene — no head-hopping
- The character can't see their own face unless looking in a mirror
- The character can't know what others are thinking — only observe behavior
- The character notices what matters to THEM, not what the plot needs noticed
- Sensory hierarchy comes from the character: a musician hears first, a painter sees first

## Interiority

Direct access to thoughts, distorted perceptions, inner contradictions. The narrator
sees inside ONE head. Interior monologue is messy, contradictory, self-deceiving —
not organized exposition.

- Thoughts are not articulate unless the character IS articulate
- The character can lie to themselves — the reader should sense the gap
- Internal monologue reveals what the character can't say — it should feel private
- Free indirect speech: blend narrator and character voice without quotation marks
- Avoid systematic internal narration after every action — let behavior speak

## Character Presence

Is this character HERE right now? Check location against timeline. Check physical
state against recent events. A character cannot be in a scene if they left two
chapters ago and haven't returned.

- Verify the character's location is consistent with previous scenes
- Verify travel time: can they physically be here given the timeline?
- Verify physical state: injuries, fatigue, clothing from last appearance
- If the character was absent: acknowledge the gap — where were they, what happened?
