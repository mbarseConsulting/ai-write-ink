# Meso Rules — qa-originality

> Scope: meso
> Loaded when: text >= 1 complete scene
> Evaluates: Scene and chapter level freshness — situations, tropes, structural choices
> Preferred output: inline or assessment

---

## Finding Axes

### situation-freshness

- **Detects**: Scene premises that have been written before. Familiar setups without unfamiliar execution. Situations where the reader knows exactly what will happen.
- **Works**: If the setup is familiar, the execution goes somewhere unexpected. The scene delivers a moment the reader hasn't experienced before — even within a known framework.
- **Fails**: A bar fight. A deathbed confession. A chase through narrow streets. And the execution follows every expected beat. Nothing surprises.
- **Fix pattern**: Name the deja-vu, propose the swerve. Keep the setup, change the angle: what would THIS character do that no other character would? What outcome would the reader not expect?
- **Example**:
  > (Classic mentor-student training scene: wise old master teaches reluctant young hero)
  > → Situation stock — le lecteur connaît chaque battement avant qu'il arrive
  > → Swerve : le mentor ment, l'élève refuse d'apprendre, l'enseignement échoue

### trope-handling

- **Detects**: Genre conventions and how they're treated — followed, subverted, or transcended. Subversion for its own sake is flagged as predictable.
- **Works**: Following conventions is fine if the texture is new. Subversion works if it reveals something. Transcendence means the trope becomes invisible because the humanity is so real.
- **Fails**: Tropes followed rigidly without new texture. Subversion that's become its own cliche ("the REAL villain was the system all along"). No awareness of convention.
- **Fix pattern**: Name the trope, assess the treatment. If following: what texture makes it fresh? If subverting: does the subversion reveal something or just invert? If unaware: make the choice conscious.
- **Example**:
  > (Love triangle resolved by choosing "neither" — a now-predictable subversion)
  > → Subversion devenue cliché — rejeter les deux est désormais attendu
  > → Trouver un dénouement qui surprend DANS le cadre du trope

### narrative-audacity

- **Detects**: Structural choices — POV decisions, information control, scene selection, temporal structure. Whether the author takes the harder, more interesting path or the easy, expected one.
- **Works**: The choice to withhold, to reveal early, to skip the expected scene, to show the aftermath instead of the event. Audacity is choosing the harder path when the easy path was available.
- **Fails**: Every structural choice is safe. Linear chronology, obvious POV, expected scene selection. The author never surprises the reader with HOW the story is told.
- **Fix pattern**: Identify the expected structural choice and propose the unexpected one. Skip the scene the reader expects. Show what the reader doesn't expect. Change the angle.
- **Example**:
  > (The big confrontation scene told in full, blow-by-blow, from the protagonist's POV)
  > → Choix structurel attendu — aucune audace narrative
  > → Et si on voyait l'après-coup ? Le point de vue du témoin ? L'ellipse ?

## Verdict Axes

### scene-coherence

- **Assesses**: Whether each scene serves a unified artistic vision or feels assembled from parts.
- **Works**: Tone, imagery, character behavior, and structural choice all serve the same intent. The scene feels authored — one hand, one vision.
- **Fails**: A scene where the tone shifts mid-way, the imagery contradicts the mood, and the character behavior serves different narrative goals. Assembled, not composed.
- **States**: unified / mostly coherent / assembled / fractured
- **Suggestion pattern**: If assembled → identify which element breaks the unity and realign it. If fractured → the scene needs to be reconceived — decide what it's ABOUT and rebuild every element to serve that.
