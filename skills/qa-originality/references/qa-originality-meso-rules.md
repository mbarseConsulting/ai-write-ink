# Meso Rules — qa-originality

> Scope: meso
> Loaded when: text >= 1 complete scene
> Evaluates: Scene and chapter level freshness — situations, tropes, structural choices
> Preferred output: inline or assessment

---

## Finding Axes

### situation-freshness

- **States**: fresh / mixed / stock
  - **fresh**: Even familiar setups deliver moments the reader hasn't experienced. Execution goes somewhere unexpected.
  - **mixed**: Some beats surprise; others follow the expected path without deviation.
  - **stock**: Setup and execution follow every expected beat. Reader knows what will happen and is right.
- **Detects**: Scene premises written before. Familiar setups without unfamiliar execution.
- **Fix pattern**: List expected beats (1, 2, 3). Swerve ONE: what would THIS character do that no other would? Rewrite that beat. 10 min.
- **Genealogy trigger**: On stock finding, name 2-3 works with same situation and beats, and 1 that swerved.
- **Example**:
  > (Mentor-student training scene) Lineage: *Star Wars*, *Karate Kid*, *Name of the Rose*.
  > Swerve: *Whiplash* — mentor is abusive, teaching is trauma, "lesson" destroys.
  > Direction: keep setup. Swerve one beat rooted in THIS story.

### trope-handling

- **States**: transcended / subverted / followed-with-texture / rigid
  - **transcended**: Trope invisible because humanity is so real. Convention dissolves into specificity.
  - **subverted**: Reversal reveals something about the material. Subversion as discovery, not reflex.
  - **followed-with-texture**: Convention followed, but every element carries a detail only THIS story could have.
  - **rigid**: Trope followed without new texture, or subversion that's become its own cliche.
- **Detects**: Genre conventions and how they're treated — followed, subverted, or transcended.
- **Fix pattern**: Name the trope and treatment. If following: give one element a detail only THIS story could have. If subverting predictably: test whether subversion reveals something. 10 min.
- **Example**:
  > (Love triangle resolved by choosing "neither" — now-predictable subversion)
  > Direction: resolution the reader cannot predict from setup. Answer from character specificity, not plot mechanics.

### narrative-audacity

- **States**: daring / mixed / safe
  - **daring**: Author takes the harder path — withholds, reveals early, skips expected scene, shows aftermath. Structural surprises.
  - **mixed**: Some structural risks coexist with default choices. Uneven ambition in HOW the story is told.
  - **safe**: Linear chronology, obvious POV, expected scene selection. No structural surprises.
- **Detects**: POV decisions, information control, scene selection, temporal structure.
- **Fix pattern**: Name the most expected structural choice. Propose one alternative. Rewrite scene opening only. 10 min.
- **Example**:
  > (Big confrontation told blow-by-blow from protagonist's POV)
  > Direction: scene opens on aftermath; confrontation reconstructed from evidence.

## Verdict Axis

### scene-coherence

- **Assesses**: Whether each scene serves a unified artistic vision or feels assembled from parts.
- **States**: unified (every element serves one artistic intent; nothing imported from a different scene) / mostly coherent (dominant unity with 1-2 elements that break it; identifiable and fixable) / assembled (multiple competent elements that don't serve the same vision; works mechanically, lacks artistic unity) / fractured (elements actively contradict each other)
- **Gating rule**: CANNOT score "unified" if any meso finding axis is at lowest state (stock/rigid/safe). Cap at "mostly coherent."
- **Suggestion pattern**:
  - assembled: Quote the element that breaks unity. Rewrite to align with scene's dominant intent. 10 min.
  - fractured: Find strongest moment. Its intent becomes scene's intent. Rewrite opening paragraph to set up that intent. 10 min.
