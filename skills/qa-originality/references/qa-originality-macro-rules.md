# Macro Rules — qa-originality

> Scope: macro | Loaded when: text >= multiple chapters or full manuscript
> Evaluates: Manuscript-level originality — concept, arc, ambition, identity | Preferred output: assessment

Verdict-dominated: originality at this scale is a global assessment.

---

## Gating Rules

- **artistic-identity** CANNOT score "irreplaceable" if concept-originality = "derivative" AND arc-originality = "formulaic". Cap at "memorable."
- **maturity** CANNOT score "masterful" if ambition = "safe" or "timid". Cap at "confident."
- **arc-originality** is independent of concept-originality. Score them separately.

---

## Verdict Axes

### concept-originality

- **Assesses**: Premise, hook, central idea. Has THIS specific combination been done?
- **States**: genuinely new (specific combination has no close precedent; reader cannot name a comparable book) / fresh combination (known ingredients, new assembly that feels different) / familiar with new texture (recognizable premise, enough authorial specificity to justify existence) / derivative (premise, structure, and treatment all have identifiable sources)
- **Genealogy trigger**: On derivative or familiar, name 2-3 works with same concept DNA and 1 that swerved.
- **Suggestion**: familiar with new texture — quote differentiating texture, replace most conventional ingredient from untouched domain, new logline, 10 min. derivative — list 3 ingredients, replace most borrowed from different genre/period, rewrite logline, 10 min.

### arc-originality

- **Assesses**: Story structure, ending, trajectories. Predictable from page 1, or invisible logic with surprise?
- **States**: surprising-inevitable (unpredictable yet in retrospect the only possible path) / some surprises (genuine swerves within conventional structure) / predictable (genre-standard beats in standard order) / formulaic (follows identifiable template, e.g. "Save the Cat beat sheet")
- **Suggestion**: predictable — map 5 beats, swerve the most predictable, rewrite in one paragraph, 10 min. formulaic — name formula, violate structurally (skip/merge/invert), new 5-beat outline, 10 min.

### ambition

- **Assesses**: Scope, difficulty, formal challenge. Is the author attempting something hard?
- **States**: ambitious (at least one major risk with no guaranteed payoff; working without a net) / competent (solid execution of known difficulty; succeeds but attempts nothing that might fail) / safe (every choice minimizes risk; no experiments, no provocation) / timid (active retreat from difficulty; visible pull-backs)
- **Suggestion**: safe — name biggest safe choice, propose dangerous alternative, write first paragraph, 10 min. timid — find pull-back, quote it, write unrestrained version, 10 min.

### artistic-identity

- **Assesses**: Overall memorability and unity of vision. Is this book irreplaceable?
- **States**: irreplaceable (occupies a space no other book occupies; remove it and the gap is visible) / memorable (strong identity that stays, but a comparable book could be named) / competent (well-made but undifferentiated; enjoy and forget) / interchangeable (swap cover with shelf neighbor, no one notices)
- **Suggestion**: competent — quote most uniquely-authored passage, name mechanism, apply to 3 openings, 10 min each. interchangeable — pull 5 paragraphs, "could this appear in another novel unnoticed?", rewrite one, 10 min.

### maturity

- **Assesses**: Confidence, control, intentionality. Does the author know what they're doing?
- **States**: masterful (every effect chosen; apparent roughness is deliberate; total control including when to let go) / confident (most effects controlled; bold choices that land; occasional misfires, intent clear) / developing (flashes of control alternating with craft-intent gaps) / uncontrolled (intent and effect diverge regularly without awareness)
- **Suggestion**: developing — quote strongest controlled passage, name technique, apply where control slips, 10 min. uncontrolled — pick scene, "intended effect?" vs "actual effect?", rewrite opening targeting intended only, 10 min.
